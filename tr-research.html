IRC §162(a): Trade or Business Expenses

26 U.S.C. §162(a)

CoCounsel Summary: Allows deductions for ordinary and necessary expenses paid or incurred in carrying on a trade or business, including certain home office expenses if principal place of business tests are met.

Citation: IRC §162(a); Treas. Reg. §1.162-1, §1.280A-2

Rev. Rul. 2020-20: Net Operating Loss Carrybacks

Rev. Rul. 2020-20

CoCounsel Insight: Clarifies application of CARES Act NOL carryback provisions, including 5-year carryback for certain tax years and interaction with §965 transition tax.

Citation: Rev. Rul. 2020-20; IRC §172; Notice 2020-26

Smith v. Commissioner – Home Office Deduction

T.C. Memo. 2013-203

CoCounsel Summary: Tax Court analyzes whether a taxpayer’s home office qualifies as principal place of business; emphasizes regular and exclusive use requirements.

Citation: Smith v. Comm’r, T.C. Memo. 2013-203; IRC §280A
